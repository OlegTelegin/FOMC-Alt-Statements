>> Alternative B 
1. The Federal Open Market Committee decided today to keep its target for the federal funds rate at 1 percent.
2. The Committee continues to believe that an accommodative stance of monetary policy, coupled with robust underlying growth in productivity, is providing important ongoing support to economic activity.
3. The evidence accumulated over the intermeeting period indicates that output is continuing to expand briskly.
4. Although new hiring has been disappointing, other indicators suggest continued improvement in the labor market. 
5. Increases in core consumer prices are muted and expected to remain low. 
6. The Committee perceives that the upside and downside risks to the attainment of susta inable growth for the next few quarters are roughly equal. 
7. The probability of an unwelcome fall in inflation has diminished in recent months and now appears almost equal to that of a rise in inflation.
8. With inflation quite low and resource use slack, the Committee believes that it can be patient in removing its policy accommodation.
