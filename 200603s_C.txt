>> Alternative C
1. The Federal Open Market Committee decided today to raise its target for the federal funds rate by 25 basis points to 4.75 percent.
2. Economic growth has rebounded in the current quarter, and the underlying pace of expansion appears to be solid.
3. In addition to increases in resource utilization, the elevated prices of energy and other commodities have the potential to add to inflation pressures going forward. As yet, however, inflation expectations remain contained.
4. The Committee judges that further policy firming may be needed to keep the risks to the attainment of both sustainable economic growth and price stability roughly in balance.
5. In any event, the Committee will respond to changes in economic prospects as needed to foster these objectives.
